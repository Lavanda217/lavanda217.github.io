Our news and exhibitions in europe

The personal exhibition of Joan Miro in the Grand Palais covers his entire creative path and shows the evolution of his work. Miro lived for a long time, and his style changed throughout his life. At the exhibition more than 150 paintings and drawings ranging from figurative Fauvist works and ending with abstractions. And there is also a sculpture and no less interesting ceramics in the spirit of surrealism, to which he returned throughout his career.

This is a kind of exhibition-expedition, which begins with Henri Rousseau's brilliant canvas “A hungry lion throws itself at the antelope.” In a world where there is almost no wildlife, we need to re-examine the relationship between humans and animals, says the description of the project. Many wrote wild animals, not knowing how they looked in reality. And in this sense, it is rather a myth about the desert, which does not make the exhibition less beautiful.
